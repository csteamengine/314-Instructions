Sleek-Traditional Paper Airplane

The following are instructions for how to create a sleek paper airplane. This design is faster than the traditional design and slightly more stable making it ideal for good distance and speed. It is slightly more difficult to make with a few more folds, however if you take your time, you can produce an excellent paper airplane. Follow the instructions below to learn how to make and launch this plane for optimal performance.

Supplies

8-1/2" X 11" Printer Paper

Optional

Paperclip-placed on front can extend flight distance.


Directions

To pause the slides, either hover the mouse over the picture or click the picture. To change slides, either click the arrows, or move the mouse away from the picture. Enjoy!

Step 1

Start by folding the paper in half lengthwise and open it out again. Be sure to line to corners up as close as you can and make a clean crisp fold.

Step 2

Place paper length ways and fold the left side to the center-line, and repeat this process for the right side. Once both sides have been folded and creased to the center-line unfold the piece of paper and open it back out.

Step 3

Now that the paper is unfolded, take the left corner and fold it to the center-line and repeat with the right side, this fold is the most commonly known fold for paper airplanes.

Step 4

Next take the left hand side and fold it towards the center again which will allow for a steeper diagonal, and repeat this process for the right side

Step 5

Repeat the previous step for an even steeper angle.

Step 6

Once both sides have been folded towards the center-line, flip the plane over so the flat side is facing up. Next, fold the plane in half on the center-line.

Step 7

Finally pop up the wings and the paper airplane is ready to fly!

Launching

To launch this plane, pinch the perpendicular piece underneath the wings and thrust it forward at a moderate speed.

XXXX WARNING XXXX

Throwing the plane too hard will result in the plane losing control and crashing, while throwing too softly will prevent the plane from being able to glide.
